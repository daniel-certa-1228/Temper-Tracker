Behavior Definitions
Antecedent: What happened before the behavior occurred.
  • Diverted Attention: Attention is briefly removed or transferred to another object or individual.
  • Demand: Request to start or stop an activity or transition to a new activity.
  • Item Removed: Toy, food, book, or other tangible taken away.
Consequence: What happened immediately following the behavior.
  • Attention: Provided with verbal or physical attention, eye contact, or physical proximity (3 feet or closer) from another person.
  • Item Given: Toy, food, book, or other tangible given or returned.
  • Item Removed: Toy, food, book, or other tangible taken away.
  • Escape: Request or activity removed without requiring completion (e.g. take speech toys away when behavior occurs).
  • None: The behavior results in no action.
Behavior: Temper tantrum
  • Yelling, screaming, or crying for 1 minute or longer that can be heard from another room within the home, through a closed door, or from outside the home.
  • Hitting, biting, kicking, pinching, scratching or otherwise hurting another individual with hands or objects within reach.
  • Property destruction to include throwing items, breaking items, or clearing surfaces that could cause permanent damage.
